Expectation from Manchester United before the Real Madrid tie was to look competitive, but eventually be outclassed, which would allow them to focus on taking back domestic dominance from Manchester City by winning the Premiere league and F.A. Cup.

But they blew everyone away.

United were a Robin Van Persie finish away from winning in Madrid and a similar half-chance away from drawing level last night.

They were dominant on the counter for 55 minutes (including an awesome own goal by the fuck up King Sergio Ramos)

Then the referee went bonkers, sending Nani off for a high boot when:
1. He was clearly going for the ball

2. THE BOOT WASN’T THAT HIGH!

Mourinho obliged with subbing in Luka Modric who brought electricity to the Real Madrid attack and capped it off with a stunning equaliser from distance.

The referee, on the other hand, continued his absolute articulate-and-detailed agenda of never ever giving United a penalty when Danny Welbeck was being harassed in the box.

Cristiano Ronaldo, oblivious to all the crap happening around him was chilling out with his former friends and out of respect for Manchester United, was having a ridiculously bad game.
